## What is Physical AI?

Physical AI refers to artificial intelligence systems that interact with the physical world through robots. Unlike traditional AI that operates in virtual environments or with abstract data, Physical AI must navigate the complexities of the real world, including physics, sensor noise, and environmental uncertainties.

Physical AI systems learn through physical interaction and experience, embodying the concept of "embodied cognition." This means that the AI's understanding is shaped not just by data processing, but by the physical form and sensory experiences of the robot.

## Humanoid Robotics

Humanoid robotics is a specialized field focused on creating robots with human-like form and capabilities. These robots often feature:
- Bipedal locomotion
- Human-like manipulation capabilities
- Social interaction abilities
- Cognitive systems that mirror human-like reasoning

Humanoid robots serve various purposes, from research platforms for understanding human cognition to practical applications in healthcare, education, and service industries.

## The Perception-Brain-Control Workflow

At the heart of every robotic system is the perception-brain-control workflow:

1. **Perception**: Sensors gather information about the environment (cameras, LIDAR, IMUs, etc.)
2. **Brain**: AI systems process sensor data, make decisions, and plan actions
3. **Control**: Motor commands are sent to actuators to execute physical actions
4. **Simulation**: Virtual environments for testing and training
5. **Deployment**: Real-world implementation and operation

This workflow represents a complete cycle where the robot continuously interacts with its environment, learns from experiences, and adapts its behavior.
